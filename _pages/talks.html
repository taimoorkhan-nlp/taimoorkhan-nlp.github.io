Talks

Title Type Venue Resources Excerpt
Reproducibility of AI/ML methods in computational social sciences talk Meta-Rep 2024, Munich, Germany The talk highlighted the prevalence of AI/ML based models in computational social science and the need to improve reusability of latest models in the area. It requires transparency, reprdoucibility and ease of reuse to communicate knowledge across discipline and to wider audience in general.
Methods Hub: A Platform for Sharing Computational Social Science Methods workshop poster CPSS KONVENS 2024, Vienna, Austria It poster elaborated on the need for a niche platform that support computationally reproducible methods for social science research.
Transformer-based Multitask Learning German Sexism Detector shared task (German sexism detection), KONVENS conference KONVENS 2024, Vienna, Austria The talk addressed German sexism detection as a multi-facet problem i.e., binary classification, multiple levels of sexism and their distribution. The proposed approach was based on a transformer based multi-task learning architecture to perform multiple classification using single model, while also benefiting from the shared common features for these classification tasks through the same architecture.
HybridDetox - A Combination of Supervised and Unsupervised Methods for Effective Multilingual Text Detoxifications proceedings talk PAN at CLEF 2024, Grenoble, France The talk proposed a hybrid text detoxification system for 9 different languages (of which only two i.e., English and Russian had parallel corpora), using a mix of supervised and unsupervised approaches.
